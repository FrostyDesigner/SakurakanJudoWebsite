FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Below is a list of questions that you may like answered before attending one of our classes.

We hope that it encourages you to come into class. We welcome everybody to drop in and watch a class or two and decide if Judo is something you are interested in. We are also happy to answer any additional questions you have that are not covered below.

Do I need to be in shape to start judo?

No. Judo is a sport that can help you develop discipline and self control.

Can you explain what judo is to someone who does not watch fights or know anything about martial arts?

Judo means "the gentle way" in Japanese. It is referred to as "gentle" because there is no striking, (punches or kicks) allowed. The thought behind this is that two players can fight and each give their best effort without injuring the other player.

Judo is an Olympic sport with its own objectives and rules. Judo is most similar to American wrestling or Brazilian jiu-jitsu. The neutral position starts from standing and involves throwing the other player to the ground.

Judo is similar to wrestling in several ways; it involves takedowns, ground fighting and pins. However in wrestling there is no choking or submissions allowed.

Judo is similar to Brazilian jiu-jitsu in several ways; it involves takedowns, ground fighting, submissions and chokes. However in jiu-jitsu there is no such thing as pins.

Do you have a table that compares judo techniques with the techniques of other martial arts?

The table below is a good reference to compare martial art styles.

Are there any prequisites for playing judo?

The first thing you learn in judo is how to fall.

We typically recommend that players have at least ten hours of ukemi (breakfall) training before live sparring. We provide the beakfall training free of charge.

So I came and watched a class, what's up with the bowing? And what is that language you are speaking?

Judo is a Japanese martial art and judo players (judoka), honor that by participating in Japanese traditions such as bowing. The techniques, commands and repetitions are also given in Japanese.

Fun Fact: Judo is one of the most popular sports in the world and everywhere judo is taught, no matter which country, it is taught using the Japanese language.

Judo allows choking? Will my child be strangled?

In most judo competitions, children are not allowed to strangle each other unless both players are over 12 years of age.

We do teach the children how to strangle and how to escape from strangles prior to the competition age to make sure they are ready in the safest way possible.

Will I Get Strangled?

Yes... eventually. Especially though if you stay after class and roll in the open mat.

What does it mean to "roll"?

It means to fight someone on the ground. This is called ne-waza in judo.

On most Wednesday nights, after class, the club turns into an open mat and every one plays Brazilian jiu-jitsu. Players from other Brazilian jiu-jits clubs will often come and wrestle and provide valuable ground work instruction. The players usually fight using Brazilian jiu-jitsu rules, which are different from judo.

Here are some rule differences you will notice.

  • Throws do not win this game so most fights start on the ground.
  • There is no such thing as pins in this game.
  • The only way to win is to submit the other player with a choke or joint lock.
  • Since there is no such thing as pins, some players will play the game on their back.
  • You are allowed to stand up and pass guard without stopping the fight.
  • Playing turtle will get you killed.
